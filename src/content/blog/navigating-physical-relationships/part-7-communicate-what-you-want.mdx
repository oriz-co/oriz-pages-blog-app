# How to Communicate What You Want

The biggest barrier to enjoying physical relationships is the fear of talking about them. We expect our partners to be mind readers, and when they aren't, we get frustrated.

## Drop the Mind-Reading Expectation

Nobody knows what feels good to you except you. And you don't know what feels good to them unless they tell you. 

If you want to take maximum enjoyment from your physical encounters, you have to use your words. 

## How to Talk About Sex

* **Do it outside the bedroom:** The best time to talk about boundaries, desires, and STI testing is over coffee or dinner, not when you are already undressed. It removes the pressure.
* **Use positive reinforcement:** Instead of saying "Don't do that," say "I really love it when you do *this* instead."
* **Be clear about your intentions:** If you only want a one-night stand, make that clear before anyone gets to the bedroom. "I think you're really attractive, but I want to be upfront that I'm not looking for anything serious right now." 

Honesty is the ultimate hack for physical enjoyment. It filters out people who want different things, and it frees you up to fully enjoy the moment with people who are on the same page.
