# Handling Rejection and Setting Boundaries

In the world of dating and physical relationships, you are going to face rejection. A lot of it. And you are going to have to reject people, too.

## How to Handle Being Rejected

Rejection stings, but it is rarely a reflection of your worth. Usually, it's just a mismatch of timing, chemistry, or intentions.
* **Take it gracefully:** If someone says no, the only correct response is "Okay, I understand." Do not argue, do not ask them to explain, and do not get angry.
* **Move on:** Walking away with your dignity intact is highly attractive. 
* **Don't internalize it:** Being rejected on Tinder or after a first date doesn't mean you are unlovable; it just means it didn't work out with *that* specific person.

## Setting Your Own Boundaries

You must protect your own peace. 
* If someone wants to hook up but refuses to use a condom, you walk away.
* If someone pressures you to go faster than you want to, you leave.
* If you are exploring your bisexuality and someone makes you feel weird or fetishized, you cut contact.

Setting a boundary isn't mean. It is the clearest way to respect yourself. "I don't feel comfortable with this, so I'm going to head home." End of discussion.
