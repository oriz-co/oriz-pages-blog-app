# Safe Sex 101 (For All Orientations)

If you are mature enough to have physical relationships, you must be mature enough to handle the responsibilities that come with them. Health and safety are paramount, regardless of whether you are hooking up with men or women.

## The Absolute Basics of Protection

* **Barrier Methods are Mandatory:** Always use condoms. They are the only method that protects against both unintended pregnancies and Sexually Transmitted Infections (STIs).
* **It applies to everything:** Condoms aren't just for penetrative sex. Depending on the acts involved, dental dams and proper hygiene are essential to prevent transmission of infections like Herpes or HPV.
* **Lubrication:** Always use water-based lubricants. Do NOT use oil-based lubes (like Vaseline or coconut oil) with latex condoms, as they will cause the condom to break.

## STI Testing

Many STIs are completely asymptomatic. You or your partner could have an infection and feel perfectly fine.
* **Get tested regularly:** If you are sexually active with multiple partners, get a full STI panel every 3-6 months.
* **It’s not a big deal:** Getting tested is a normal part of adult life. You can visit private clinics or use services like Apollo Diagnostics or Dr. Lal PathLabs for discreet testing in Bhubaneswar.

## Navigating Safe Sex as a Bisexual Man

Since you are exploring bisexuality, it is crucial to understand that safe sex practices apply across all genders. 
* If you are having sex with men, understand that the risk profile for certain STIs (like HIV) can be different, making condom use and regular testing absolutely critical.
* Look into PrEP (Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis) if you are actively engaging with multiple male partners.
* Always communicate openly with your partners about your STI status and when you were last tested. It shows immense maturity and builds instant trust.
