# The Emotional Side of Casual Intimacy

There is a massive misconception that you can easily separate physical intimacy from emotional attachment. For a few people, this is true. For the vast majority, hormones like oxytocin (the "cuddle hormone") make "no strings attached" very difficult.

## Catching Feelings

If you engage in a casual physical relationship, someone usually catches feelings. Sometimes it's them; sometimes it's you.

**If you catch feelings:**
You need to communicate it. Don't pretend you are fine with casual sex if you are actually waiting for them to fall in love with you. It will destroy your self-esteem.

**If they catch feelings:**
You cannot "take advantage" of their feelings just to keep getting physical enjoyment. If you know a partner has fallen for you and you do not feel the same way, the ethical thing to do is to step back and end the physical arrangement. Continuing to sleep with someone who wants more from you is cruel.

## The "Friends with Benefits" Trap

FWB situations sound perfect on paper but are the hardest to maintain. They require:
* Brutally honest communication.
* Strict boundaries (e.g., no sleeping over, no meeting each other's families).
* An exit strategy for when one of you starts dating someone seriously.

If you want to enjoy life without causing trauma to yourself or others, handle the emotional side of physical relationships with immense care.
