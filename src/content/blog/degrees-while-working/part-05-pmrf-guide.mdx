## What Is PMRF?

The Prime Minister's Research Fellowship was launched by the
Government of India in 2018. The goal is to attract the best
B.Tech/BS graduates into research by offering competitive
fellowships that rival private sector salaries.

PMRF fellows are those top-tier students who might otherwise
go directly into industry (Google, Microsoft, TCS, etc.) but
choose to do a PhD because the financial package is competitive.

### Why PMRF Exists

India's biggest research challenge is that its brightest
engineering graduates choose industry over research. PMRF
tries to fix this by paying PhDs ₹70k–80k/month — which is
comparable to a good entry-level software engineering salary.

## PMRF Stipend — Full Breakdown

### Monthly Stipend

| Year | Monthly Stipend | Monthly HRA (Z-city 8%) | Total Monthly |
|:--|:--|:--|:--|
| **Year 1** | ₹70,000 | ₹5,600 | ~₹75,600 |
| **Year 2** | ₹70,000 | ₹5,600 | ~₹75,600 |
| **Year 3** | ₹75,000 | ₹6,000 | ~₹81,000 |
| **Year 4** | ₹80,000 | ₹6,400 | ~₹86,400 |
| **Year 5** | ₹80,000 | ₹6,400 | ~₹86,400 |

*HRA varies by city. IIT campus towns are typically Z-category
cities. IIT Delhi (Delhi) and IIT Bombay (Mumbai) get higher HRA.*

### Annual Research Grant

Every PMRF fellow gets **₹2,00,000 per year** for research
expenses. This covers:
- Travel to conferences in India and abroad
- Research equipment, software
- Books and journals
- Lab materials

**Total over 5 years:** ₹10,00,000 in research grants alone.

### Total Financial Package Over 5 Years

| Item | Total |
|:--|:--|
| Stipend over 5 years | ₹46,20,000 |
| Research grants over 5 years | ₹10,00,000 |
| HRA over 5 years | ~₹3,80,000 |
| **Grand Total** | **~₹60,00,000** |

₹60 lakhs over 5 years for doing a PhD in India. This is
comparable to many mid-level software engineering jobs.

## PMRF Eligibility — The Strict Requirements

### Requirement 1: Qualifying Institution

You must have completed your qualifying degree from one of these:
- Any of the **23 IITs** (Indian Institutes of Technology)
- **IISc** (Indian Institute of Science)
- Any of the **5 IISERs** (Indian Institutes of Science Education
  and Research)
- **NITs** (National Institutes of Technology)
- **IIITs** (Indian Institutes of Information Technology — National
  Importance category only: Allahabad, Hyderabad, Bangalore,
  Gwalior, Jabalpur, Kancheepuram, Kurnool, Pune, Srirangam)
- Central institutions like **IIT(ISM)** Dhanbad
- Other Centrally Funded Technical Institutions (CFTIs)

> **Critical Note:** If you graduated from RKGIT (affiliated with
> AKTU/state university), you are **NOT directly eligible** for PMRF
> under the standard track. RKGIT is a private engineering college
> affiliated with a state university, not a CFTI.

### Requirement 2: Minimum CGPA

- **CGPA ≥ 8.0** on a 10-point scale
- OR equivalent marks if your institution does not use CGPA

### Requirement 3: PhD Program Enrollment

You must be enrolled in a **full-time PhD program** at any
of the PMRF-granting institutions (IITs, IISc, IISERs, select NITs).

### Requirement 4: Timing

- **Direct Entry:** Applied within 2 years of completing B.Tech
  (without Master's) or within 1 year (with Master's).
- **Lateral Entry:** Already enrolled in PhD for ≤ 12 months
  (with Master's) or ≤ 24 months (without Master's).

## Can RKGIT Graduates Apply for PMRF?

**The Honest Answer:**

Directly: **No.** RKGIT is affiliated with AKTU, which is a
state university. PMRF currently requires graduation from
CFTIs (Central Government institutions).

**But here is a workaround:**

1. Complete B.Tech from RKGIT (done).
2. Complete an **M.Tech from an IIT or NIT** (GATE required).
3. Apply for PhD at an IIT after M.Tech.
4. Apply for PMRF lateral entry during PhD (M.Tech holders
   have a better shot at lateral PMRF).

This is a longer path (B.Tech → M.Tech → PhD → PMRF) but
is achievable.

**Alternative:** If you have an extremely outstanding B.Tech
performance (9.5+ CGPA, publications, Olympiad achievements)
and strong recommendations, some institutions may consider you
for their research programs that feed into PMRF.

## PMRF Selection Process

### Two Annual Cycles

**May Cycle:**
- Applications: Usually February–April
- Interview/Selection: May–June
- Fellowship Start: July

**December Cycle:**
- Applications: Usually September–November
- Interview/Selection: November–December
- Fellowship Start: January

### Selection Steps

**Step 1: Apply on PMRF Portal**
- Visit: https://pmrf.in
- Create account and fill the application form.
- Upload: Academic transcripts, CGPA/marks, GATE score,
  research publications (if any), supervisor details.

**Step 2: Departmental Shortlisting**
- The department at your PhD institution shortlists candidates.
- Your supervisor (PhD guide) must agree to guide you.

**Step 3: National Expert Committee Review**
- Shortlisted applications go to a national committee.
- Review: Academic performance, research potential, SOP.

**Step 4: Selection**
- ~900 fellows selected per year across India.
- Offer letter sent via PMRF portal.

## PMRF 2.0 — Updates and Expansion

The government expanded PMRF in 2023-24 (PMRF 2.0):

- **More seats:** Increased from 3,000 to higher capacity.
- **More institutions:** More CFTIs included.
- **Higher stipend:** Revised upward (from previous ₹31k–41k).
- **Research grant:** Increased to ₹2L/year.
- **Digital disbursement:** Direct transfer to bank accounts.

## Life as a PMRF Fellow

### What Your Average Month Looks Like

**Income:**
- Stipend: ₹70,000–₹80,000
- HRA: ₹5,600–₹19,200 (depends on city)
- Research grant (amortized monthly): ~₹16,667
- **Total effective income:** ~₹90,000–₹1,15,000/month

**Expenses at IIT Campus:**
- Hostel + Mess: ₹5,000–₹10,000
- Personal: ₹5,000–₹10,000
- Total: ~₹10,000–₹20,000

**Monthly Savings Possible:** ₹70,000–₹95,000

This makes PMRF comparable to a 3–5 year experienced software
engineer salary in many Indian cities.

### Research Expectations

PMRF fellows are expected to:
- Publish in top-tier conferences/journals (IEEE, ACM, etc.)
- Present at international conferences (funded by research grant)
- Complete thesis within 5 years
- Report progress biannually

## FAQs

### Q: I am 23 with B.Tech from RKGIT. Is PMRF completely out of reach?

**A:** Not completely. The path is longer:

Option 1 (7–8 years):
- GATE → IIT M.Tech (2 years) → PhD at IIT → Apply for PMRF
  lateral entry during PhD → ₹70k–80k fellowship

Option 2 (5–6 years):
- GATE → Direct PhD at IIT (no M.Tech) → Apply for lateral
  PMRF during PhD

If you have exceptional academic performance or research, it
is worth pursuing.

### Q: How many PMRF fellows are there from CSE?

**A:** CSE and related engineering fields are the most common
domains. IIT Delhi, IIT Bombay, IIT Madras, and IISc have the
highest number of PMRF fellows.

### Q: Can I work part-time or as a freelancer while on PMRF?

**A:** PMRF is for full-time PhD students. The regulations
prohibit taking up regular employment. However, academic
consulting (reviewing papers, occasional teaching) is generally
not prohibited. Check specific terms with your institution.

### Q: Is PMRF stipend taxable?

**A:** Currently, PhD fellowships under the ₹5 lakh annual
exemption limit are generally not taxable. The ₹70k–80k/month
stipend (~₹8.4L–9.6L/year) may be taxable above a threshold.
Consult a tax professional for your specific situation.
